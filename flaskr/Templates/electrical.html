A brain needs a body to operate!
Our device's electrical portion consists of PCBs, motors, drivers, passive/active components, wires, and of course, the Arduino Due

Main PCB

The main PCB is responsible for receiving a 12V input from the 120ACV-12DCV and distributing the accurate voltage and current to all the components through voltage regulators and passive components
It is also the housing for the major components such as the Arduino Due, power connector, and motor drivers.

Download

User Interface PCB

The sole purpose of the UI PCB was to act as a holding station for the UI components such as the LCD, Multifunction Knob, Power Switch, and LED power indicator.
This PCB does not contain and passive components as all power regulations are done in the Main PCB which connects to this board with one ribbon cable.

Initial Wiring Diagram

Initially, during prototype phase, we focused on the idea that both be connected from one part only hence the idea of male-female connections shown below. We later realized that it is impractical to debug and setup, so we went with the ribbon cable connection.
Also, at this point of the project, the DC motor is not included as it was a stretch goal. It is also worth to mention that the motor did not fit as intended in the inclosure, so we had to adjust the Main PCB to account for the Stepper motor housing.

Final Wiring Diagram

After all the necessary adjustment, the figure below shows the final wiring diagram of the project with all the edge cases being accounted for.
The connection to the 120ACV-12DCV adapter is not shown in the diagram below because it is oriented to the right side of the device.
It can be noticed that the DC motor wires look off, but they are actually routed through their designated tunnel.
